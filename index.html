~/trans

Transitioning can be one of the most challening things a person will ever do, mentally. The pressure of coming out to everyone, not knowing whether their reactions will be positive or negative. Starting a life-long medication plan just to be yourself. Not even talking about the whole "accepting the truth" at the beginning.

Before starting your transition - and even during it - many questions may arise. How do I transition in my country? What options are available to me medically? Is my doctor prescribing the right dosage? What about potential risks, any studies? And what the heck is a DIY?

I'm not here to sow doubt. The good news is, milions of people are just like you and for better or worse, have gone through the same feelings and hurdles as you and have created many resources dedicated to helping other trans people.

Before you go scouring the web for dosages and what not, remember that each and every person will need to do HRT slightly differently. For some, pills just don't work (like for me!). Some need to take 8 mg / day, others only 4 mg. What every website and person will they you is that what really matters are the resulting levels - estradiol above 100 pg/mL and testosterone below 50 ng/dL. The dosages that you will find are supposed to start you off on something and your job is to go and get a bloodtest(s) and adjust your dosage accordingly!

General information

  • The DIY HRT Directory 2.0 - Don't let the name fool you! Apart from hosting DIY related information, it provides information surrounding available forms of estradiol/testosterone and their respective dosages.
  • Transfeminine Science - Site dedicated to providing studies surrounding transfeminine people (including a great injectable estradiol simulator).

DIY resources

  • HRT Cafe - Great repository of vendors selling both pharmaceutical grade and homebrewed HRT.

Forums

  • r/trans - A subreddit for general discussion about trans related topics.
  • r/asktransgender - A subreddit dedicated for asking questions about transitioning, for trans folk and their relatives, partners and/or friends.
